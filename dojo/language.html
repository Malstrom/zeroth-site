The problem

You’re not a beginner. You just have gaps.

Standard courses reset you to zero regardless of what you already know. You repeat content you’ve mastered and never touch the specific areas where you actually break down.

The result: months of study with no real improvement in the conversations that matter — the ones under pressure, on unfamiliar topics, or with native speakers who don’t slow down.

The problem isn’t effort. It’s that no one mapped your real gaps and built a session around them.

How it works

Five rules dojo never breaks

01

Gap-first, always

Every session starts from your open gaps, not from the next lesson in a sequence. If you have no gaps on record, the first session builds the map.

02

No repetition of mastered content

Once a gap closes, dojo moves on. It does not review what you already know to fill session time.

03

Time is a constraint, not a filler

You say how long you have. dojo fits the session to the time — no overrun, no “just five more minutes.”

04

Real contexts only

Exercises simulate the situations where you actually break down — job interviews, phone calls, debates — not abstract grammar drills.

05

Continuity across sessions

dojo remembers everything. Progress, failures, patterns. Each session continues exactly where the last one stopped.
